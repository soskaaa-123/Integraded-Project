Creative Writing - The Big Game

Last weekend was the most exciting day of my life. Our school football team, the Eagles, played against the Tigers in the championship final. Everyone at school was talking about the game. My friends and I wore our Eagles jerseys and sat in the front row of the bleachers. The game started at 3 PM, and the stadium was full of cheering fans.


In the first half, the Tigers scored a quick goal, and we were all worried. But our captain, Omar, never gave up. He encouraged the team to keep playing hard. In the second half, the Eagles played much better. Omar scored an amazing goal from the halfway line! The crowd went wild! Then, in the last minute, our striker Khalid scored the winning goal. The final score was 2-1!


The celebrations were incredible. Everyone ran onto the field to hug the players. We lifted Omar and Khalid onto our shoulders. The coach was crying with happiness. We took hundreds of photos together. After the game, the school organized a big party in the gym. We ate pizza, danced, and talked about every moment of the match. It was the best day ever!

🌍 Social Studies

History & Geography

About This Subject

Social Studies combines History, Geography, and Civic Education. Our Integrated Project explores how pandemics have shaped human history and how society responds to health crises.

Flu vs COVID-19

Similarities: Both are respiratory viruses spread through droplets. They cause fever, cough, and fatigue.


Differences: COVID-19 spreads more easily, has a longer incubation period (2-14 days vs 1-4 days for flu), and can cause more severe complications like blood clots and long-term symptoms. COVID-19 has a higher mortality rate, especially among elderly and those with pre-existing conditions.

Prevention Tips

  • Wash hands frequently with soap and water
  • Wear masks in crowded indoor spaces
  • Maintain social distancing (1-2 meters)
  • Get vaccinated against both flu and COVID-19
  • Stay home if you feel sick
  • Keep rooms well-ventilated
  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces

Healthy Daily Schedule During Pandemic

A structured daily routine helps maintain physical and mental health during isolation. Key elements include: regular sleep schedule, balanced meals, exercise, online learning, family time, and relaxation activities like reading or listening to music.

Français

À propos de cette matière

Le français est une langue romante parlée par plus de 300 millions de personnes dans le monde. Dans notre projet intégré, nous explorons la langue française à travers le dialogue, la grammaire et la culture.

Dialogue - La Routine Quotidienne

Léa: Bonjour Marie, Comment ça va? (Hello Marie, how are you?)


Marie: Ça va bien Merci, Et toi? (I'm fine thanks, and you?)


Léa: Qu'est-ce que tu fais pendant ta routine quotidienne? (What do you do in your daily routine?)


Marie: Je me réveille à sept heures, je prends mon petit déjeuner, puis je vais à l'école. L'après-midi, je fais mes devoirs et je joue avec mes amis. Le soir, je dîne avec ma famille et je me couche à vingt-et-une heures. (I wake up at 7, have breakfast, then go to school. In the afternoon, I do homework and play with friends. In the evening, I have dinner with family and go to bed at 9 PM.)

Vocabulary

  • La routine quotidienne = Daily routine
  • Se réveiller = To wake up
  • Le petit déjeuner = Breakfast
  • Les devoirs = Homework
  • Le soir = Evening
  • Se coucher = To go to bed

Deutsch

Über dieses Fach

Deutsch ist die Sprache, die von über 100 Millionen Menschen gesprochen wird. In unserem integrierten Projekt erkunden wir die deutsche Sprache durch Gespräche, Grammatik und Kultur.

Gesunder Tagesablauf während der Pandemie

  • 07:00 — Aufstehen & Frühstück
    Ich wache auf und frühstücke. Ich esse Obst oder Müsli.
    Wake up and have breakfast
  • 08:00–12:00 — Schule / Lernen
    Ich mache Online-Unterricht oder Hausaufgaben.
    Online classes or homework
  • 12:00–13:00 — Mittagessen
    Ich esse gesundes Essen. Danach mache ich eine Pause.
    Eat healthy food, then take a break
  • 13:00–15:00 — Sport / Bewegung
    Ich gehe spazieren, jogge oder mache Übungen.
    Go walking, jogging, or do exercises
  • 15:00–17:00 — Lernen / Projekte
    Ich übe Fächer oder arbeite an Projekten.
    Practice subjects or work on projects
  • 17:00–18:00 — Freizeit
    Ich lese, höre Musik oder spiele.
    Read, listen to music, or play
  • 18:00–19:00 — Abendessen
    Ich esse mit meiner Familie und wir reden über den Tag.
    Dinner with family, discuss the day
  • 19:00–20:00 — Entspannung
    Ich meditiere oder schreibe Tagebuch.
    Meditation or journaling
  • 20:00–21:00 — Vorbereitung für morgen
    Ich organisiere meine Sachen und lese ein wenig.
    Prepare for tomorrow, read a bit
  • 21:30 — Schlafenszeit
    Ich gehe schlafen, um gesund zu bleiben.
    Go to sleep to stay healthy

Wichtige Vokabeln

  • der Tagesablauf = daily routine
  • frühstücken = to have breakfast
  • gesund = healthy
  • die Bewegung = exercise
  • die Freizeit = free time
  • sich entspannen = to relax

Flu vs COVID-19 Comparison

Understanding the key differences and similarities between influenza and the coronavirus helps us take better precautions and stay healthy.

Key Differences

Feature Flu COVID-19
Incubation Period 1-4 days 2-14 days
Spread Rate Moderate Spreads more easily and quickly
Symptoms Fever, cough, fatigue Fever, cough, fatigue; may cause loss of taste/smell
Complications Standard respiratory issues Blood clots, long-term effects
Mortality Rate Lower Higher fatality rate overall
Vaccine Annual vaccine Vaccine with boosters for variants

Common Prevention

Both viruses can be prevented through hand hygiene, mask-wearing in crowded spaces, vaccination, staying home when sick, and maintaining good ventilation indoors.
